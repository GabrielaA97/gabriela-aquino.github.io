Nowcasting GDP in Times of Crisis and Non-Crisis: A Comparative Study of Econometric and Machine Learning Models in El Salvador
Nowcasting del PIB en tiempos de crisis y no crisis: un estudio comparativo de modelos econométricos y de machine learning en El Salvador
MSc Dissertation — University of Liverpool · September 2025 · Supervisor: Dr. Mohamed Doukali
This paper provides the first vintage-based, regime-stratified comparison of econometric and machine learning nowcasters for El Salvador's quarterly GDP. Using a mixed-frequency panel of 123 monthly and quarterly indicators (2005–2024) with ragged-edge real-time evaluation across five within-quarter vintages, I find that Ridge regression delivers the strongest gains during crisis (avg. RMSE ratio 0.73 vs ARMA; 0.62 at the month-of vintage), consistent with L2 shrinkage preserving grouped signals when indicators co-move under stress. In tranquil periods, ARMA and Dynamic Factor Models match or outperform all ML alternatives — supporting a state-contingent nowcasting protocol for data-constrained policy institutions.

Impact of Family Remittances on the Economic Growth of El Salvador: An Analysis of Their Productive Use
Impacto de las remesas familiares en el crecimiento económico de El Salvador: un análisis de su uso productivo
Policy Paper · Central Reserve Bank of El Salvador
This study examines the short- and long-run relationship between remittance inflows and GDP growth in El Salvador using a Vector Error Correction (VEC) model within an extended Cobb-Douglas production framework incorporating GDP, capital stock, employment, and remittances. Following Johansen cointegration tests confirming a stable long-run relationship, the estimated long-run elasticity of GDP with respect to remittances is 0.13%, with an error correction coefficient of −0.66 (66% annual adjustment speed). The model explains 70.6% of GDP variation (adjusted R²). Results indicate that while remittances are predominantly directed toward consumption, their productive channelling — particularly into human capital and investment — represents a pathway through which their aggregate growth impact could be amplified.

Estimation of Female Sectoral Employment and Wages in El Salvador, 1990–2015: An Input-Output Approach
Estimación del empleo y salario sectorial femenino en El Salvador, 1990–2015: un enfoque desde el insumo-producto
Undergraduate Thesis — Universidad Centroamericana José Simeón Cañas · October 2020
With Sofía Hernández Palacios, Zayda Ortiz Batres & Ana Laura Pérez Gómez · Advisor: Dr. Mario César Sánchez Pérez
This paper analyses gender-based occupational segregation in El Salvador across 45 economic sectors over 1990–2015, using Wiedmann's Total Impact Multipliers (TIMs) methodology applied to the Leontief Input-Output Matrix. The TIMs approach expands employment and wage data from 14 aggregated sectors to 45 disaggregated branches, enabling a granular analysis of labour market structure. Drawing on the national household survey (EHPM), findings reveal persistent horizontal segregation: women remain concentrated in fewer than 5 of 45 sectors throughout the period, with limited intersectoral mobility. Gender wage gaps are documented across educational levels and economic branches, contributing new statistical evidence on structural inequality in Central American labour markets.

An Approach to the Measurement of Cross-Border Digital Trade in El Salvador
Un enfoque para la medición del comercio digital transfronterizo en El Salvador
Policy Paper · Presented at UNCTAD E-Commerce Working Group, Geneva · Central Reserve Bank of El Salvador
Develops El Salvador's first official statistical framework for measuring cross-border digital trade, using Python-based transaction-level analysis of 20M+ annual credit and debit card records. The methodology maps digital transactions to standard Balance of Payments categories, generating the first quantitative estimates of digital platforms' contribution to GDP and the trade balance. El Salvador was one of only two countries presenting transaction-level analysis at the UNCTAD forum; the methodology is now referenced as regional best practice.
